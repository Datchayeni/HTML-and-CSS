In an interview with The Paris Review, Ernest Hemingway famously said,When you stop doing things for fun, you might as well be dead. This idea resonates deeply in classic literature, such as Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen, where characters navigate societal expectations while seeking personal happiness.
