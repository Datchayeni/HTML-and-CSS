Astronomers have discovered that our solar system traveled through a dense, star-forming region near Orion about 14 million years ago. This cosmic journey may have altered Earth’s environment, introducing interstellar dust that left traces in geological records. The findings offer new insight into how the galaxy’s motion influences planetary history.
